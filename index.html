Strategic Objectives

Western Region — 2027 & Beyond
01
High Priority

Market Presence in Jeddah

Simultaneous launch of both concepts in 2027, establishing a strong dual-brand footprint in the Western Region's primary market.

02
High Priority

Multi-Segment Targeting

Sobhy Kaber (Fine Dining) targets the premium evening segment; La Venue (All-Day Dining) captures breakfast-through-dinner across wider demographics.

03
High Priority

Achieve ROI ≤ 10 Years

Daily cover target of ≥ 880 combined orders (330 operating days) to ensure the base-case payback timeline is met or exceeded.

04
Medium Priority

Customer Loyalty & Retention

Loyalty programmes, terrace dining experience, and active delivery app partnerships (Jahez, HungerStation) to maximise repeat customer rate.

05
Medium Priority

Operational Excellence

Unified quality benchmarks and a centralised training team across both branches to protect brand integrity and reduce operational variance.

06
Future Phase

Next-Phase Regional Expansion

Makkah, Madinah, and Taif within 3 years of Jeddah stabilisation. Feasibility study to commence Q4 2027 once both branches reach steady-state.

7

Risk Register

Key Risks & Mitigation Measures

Rising Operating Costs

Medium

Weekly food-cost tracking tied to POS; fixed-price supplier contracts locked before launch to hedge against input cost inflation.

Low Occupancy Rate

High

Structured soft opening (invitees & media) followed by a full marketing launch. 6-month occupancy KPI reviewed weekly by the Operations Director.

Construction & Fit-Out Delays

Medium

Strict milestone schedule with contractual penalty clauses. Bi-weekly site progress reviews by the Project Manager. Buffer built into pre-opening timeline.

Competitive Pressure in Jeddah

Low

Clear brand differentiation through unique concepts, quality consistency, and the complete dining experience (terrace, service, menu innovation).

Strategic Recommendations

Implementation Priorities for Management
1

Drive Full Occupancy in First 6 Months

Target corporate events, large Jeddah families, and social media influencers to accelerate brand awareness. Occupancy in this period is the single largest lever for closing the payback gap.

2

Monitor Food Cost Daily

POS system linked to inventory management. Weekly food-cost report issued every Sunday to the CEO. The 30% target must be treated as a ceiling, not a guideline.

3

Delivery as an Additional Revenue Stream

Pre-launch agreements with Jahez and HungerStation secured before opening day. Delivery accounts for 130 of the 880 combined daily covers — a material contributor to revenue.

4

Loyalty Programme from Day One

Points system, Ramadan promotions, and birthday rewards to be operational at launch. Early loyalty sign-ups should be a tracked KPI in the first 90 days.

5

Monthly Financial Reporting to the Board

KPI dashboard comparing actual vs. budgeted P&L, submitted by the 10th of each month. Variance exceeding ±10% on any major cost line triggers an immediate review meeting.

6

Prepare for Makkah & Madinah Expansion

Feasibility study to commence Q4 2027, once the Jeddah operations reach 6 months of steady-state performance. The Western Region strategy positions Jeddah as the operational blueprint for all subsequent branches.
